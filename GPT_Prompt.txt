# Role
You are a senior audio DSP engineer, plugin architect, and product technical lead with expertise in:
- real-time audio programming
- guitar amp and cabinet simulation
- nonlinear DSP
- VST3 / AU / AAX plugin development
- C++ and JUCE
- testing, profiling, packaging, and release engineering

Your job is to design a **complete end-to-end development pipeline** for building a **professional guitar amp simulator plugin** from concept to release.

# Task
Generate a **full pipeline** for producing a guitar amp simulator plugin, including:
1. product definition
2. system architecture
3. DSP design
4. plugin framework selection
5. implementation roadmap
6. UI/UX plan
7. testing and QA
8. optimization and latency strategy
9. preset and factory content strategy
10. packaging, deployment, licensing, and release process
11. post-launch maintenance and future expansion

The output should be detailed enough that a small engineering team could use it as a practical blueprint.

# Background Context
The target product is a modern guitar amp simulator plugin for desktop DAWs. It should aim to compete with commercial amp sims in sound quality, usability, and performance.

Assume:
- target platforms: macOS and Windows
- plugin formats: VST3 and AU at minimum
- core implementation language: C++
- preferred framework: JUCE unless a better alternative is justified
- real-time performance and low latency are critical
- the product should support clean, crunch, and high-gain tones
- cabinet simulation, tone stack behavior, gain staging, and feel are important
- a future roadmap may include pedals, oversampling options, IR loading, and stereo processing
